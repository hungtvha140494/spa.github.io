Cold sauna

If you have ever been to a community relaxation spa, you must have experienced a cold snow sauna after going through an infrared sauna, rock salt, herbs. This is a typical example of a cold sauna model that is still quite new and unique.

Accordingly, cold sauna is considered as the final step in relaxing sauna therapy and health care. Cold sauna is the process of helping the body's body temperature return to normal after a hot sauna. With a room temperature of about 0 - 5 degrees Celsius, cold sauna helps improve blood circulation, tighten pores, and restore damaged skin. In particular, when sitting in a cold sauna, the steamer will experience a very new and special feeling. Cold air will spread to create a chill down the spine, going from hot to cold also causes the body to experience a slight shock, a slight loss of body temperature until it reaches equilibrium.
